## How proposals are evaluated

Three questions. Maintainers answer them on the proposal thread. No scoring, no totals, no weights.

### 1. Does this make sense for Zcash beyond payments?

Is the proposal working on something that expands what people can build on Zcash, or is it adjacent to payments plumbing that others already own? We lean toward the application layer: identity, credit, governance, attestations, compliance tooling, audits. If it is unclear, the thread is where we figure it out.

### 2. Can this ship in 2 to 4 weeks?

Scope, not novelty. A research problem dressed as a sprint gets pushed back for rescoping, not rejected. We would rather take something smaller and concrete than something grand and vague.

### 3. Does the proposer have a plan for after the sprint?

"Keep building it myself", "leave as reference", "apply for a grant somewhere else" are all valid answers. "I have not thought about it" is a signal to talk it through on the thread before accepting, not a rejection.

## What we explicitly do not evaluate

- Market size
- Token economics
- Community growth
- Marketing plan

We ship code. Anything downstream is the proposer's call.

## If a maintainer says no

Rejections come with a reason and, when possible, a shape the proposal could take to be accepted. Disagreement is fine: [open a Discussion](https://github.com/ZcashApplicationsLab/lab/discussions) or rework the proposal and reopen.
